Travel to: Istanbul, Turkey

[History]

Istanbul's past name was Byzantium, the city was the capital of the Byzantine Empire.
The city was sieged multiple times and all of them in failure by mutliple Islamic powers.
All but the final one, this siege was by the Ottoman Empire and lasted 55 days.
Sultan Mehmed II of the Ottoman Empire conquered the city, which he believed it was his destiny.
Remnants of this time include The Walls of Constantinople (Istanbul) which still stand today.

[Culture]

Istanbul's population consists mostly of muslims, though it's a mix betwen jewish, greek, and armenian populations.
Of course, Islam is this city's most popular religion as it has mosques for praying, and especially since Turkey's flag is the symbol of Islam.
The mosques built in Istanbul include, the Hagia Sophia, Sultanahmet, and the Yeni Cami which is new, although there are much more mosques.
This city has Middle Eastern along with Western cultures together, there are merchants, and fashionable botiques, people have many diverse backgrounds.
A tradition in Istanbul is the drummer of Ramadan, this drummer served as an alarm as people needed to know when to wake up to eat their meal before fasting.
